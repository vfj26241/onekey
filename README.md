## What Makes OneKey Different

Let's skip the usual marketing speak. Here's what actually stands out:

**Open-source firmware.** The hardware is audited by SlowMist. The code lives on GitHub. Anyone can look. The only closed part is the secure element chip itself — standard practice across the industry, because chip manufacturers require it.

**SignGuard — their anti-blind-signing system.** This is the one that matters in 2026. Blind signing (approving a transaction without actually reading what you're signing) is how most crypto gets drained. OneKey's SignGuard parses the transaction into plain English on *both* the companion app and the device screen independently. So even if your computer is compromised, the wallet itself tells you what you're actually approving. Phishing contracts get flagged. Malicious approvals get flagged. It's the kind of thing that sounds obvious but is surprisingly rare.

**Four EAL6+ secure elements on the Pro.** Most wallets have one. Some have two. The OneKey Pro uses four bank-grade chips in parallel — the theory being that even if one is somehow compromised, the others remain intact.

**No KYC, no data collection.** Your keys, your crypto. They don't collect private data, don't run KYC on purchases, and the app doesn't need an account to function.
